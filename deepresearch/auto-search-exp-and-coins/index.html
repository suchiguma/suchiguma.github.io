Assumptions

We simulated the auto search algorithm and enemy spawns for each campaign stage 100 000 times using one fleet Chapters 1 to 15 and 100 000 times using two fleets Chapters 9 to 15 The server decides what when and where enemies spawn We made several reasonable assumptions and the simulated results were close enough to empirical results for a few stages chosen for verification Assumptions For Two Fleets the player set Roles for the mob fleet to Escorts and the boss fleet to Flagships For One Fleet the player set Roles for his her primary fleet to All Enemies and the throwaway statpadding fleet to Standby Submarines are not summoned in battle so their oil cost is zero The data found in the client matches what the server uses as parameters for generating enemies Fleets spawn in any valid non blocked tile with equal probability This includes player fleets normal enemies elite enemies and the boss The auto search pathfinding algorithm is done in the client so we did not need to make an assumption See Coin Details for further assumptions about clearing rewards and per battle drops For Chapter 8 and below there is no oil cap so we assumed 67 oil per battle for a typical max limit broken new player fleet

Explanations

When the enemy boss fleet spawns your Flagships or All Enemies fleet will go fight it But what happens if all paths to the boss are blocked by an enemy mob fleet Your fleet will take the path with the fewest enemy fleets Notably if you are using mob and boss fleet roles your boss fleet will fight the extra enemy mobs plus the enemy boss Note Extra mobs that block the boss have several mostly negative effects on efficiency Your boss fleet may lose too much HP before the boss battle causing a lower rank like A or B or even outright losing the battle Lower mob exp oil You pay more oil per sortie for the same amount of mob fleet experience Higher boss exp oil This is the only good effect but requires that your boss fleet ships all need experience and that every battle is still S rank Significantly lower coins oil You pay more oil per sortie but the clearing rewards don t increase Ammo Pickup Change 2025 On November 6 2025 the developers made auto search go for ammo supply question mark and flare tiles Because going to those tiles changes the mob fleet s position the distribution and number of enemy fleets you fight have changed for some stages Turns out the only significant effect is that in 9 4 the average number of mob battles increased by 0 23 for both one fleet and two fleets which is a nerf in terms of rewards The types of tiles that auto search will pick up are defined by stage Currently it goes for ammo then question marks and flares in Chapters 9 and up and it ignores these tiles in Chapters 8 and below Here is how auto search currently works for Chapter 9 and higher If the fleet is 3 or more ammo below the maximum ammo and there is a clear path to an ammo supply tile then go to the ammo Clear path means no blocking enemies so the fleet can get there in one turn If there is a clear path to a question mark or flare tile then go to it If there is a boss then use the boss fleet to go fight it if the path is clear If there s no clear path then use the boss fleet to start working on the fewest number of enemy fleets that will clear a path to the boss Otherwise use the mob fleet to fight the nearest enemy Note We have simulated the fleet positions when picking up question mark and flare tiles but not the coins tech packs or cargo fleet that can spawn from question marks Curious readers may check the tables below for how many question mark tiles are picked up

Coin Details

Chapter 9 and higher have Clearing Rewards that provide coins gear enhancement plates dorm food cognitive chips and augment module experience after each successful sortie More oil spent equals more coins from clearing rewards up to the oil cap All results for Chapter 9 on this page assume your fleets are expensive enough to reach oil cap The clearing reward coin count for each stage is taken from this CN wiki source but we do not condone their data collection methods Data collected by EN players are close enough to these numbers Each battle drops some coins and has a chance to drop a ship The averages are copied from the same CN wiki source We assumed that for stages with multiple bosses the non final bosses have the same drop rates as event elite enemies We further assumed that the average coins per retired ship including gear is 20 coins The coins per ship actually depends on the probability distribution of different ships in each stage but a blanket assumption of 20 coins should be close enough to truth Again data collected by EN players are close enough to these numbers
